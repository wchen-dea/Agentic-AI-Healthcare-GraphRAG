# Business Specifications

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document defines the business context, use cases, stakeholders, capabilities, and
operational constraints for the Healthcare Agentic AI GraphRAG platform.

**What this platform is:**  
A healthcare intelligence blueprint that ingests real-time clinical events, enriches them
with master data, stores dual evidence (semantic vectors + clinical relationships), and
serves grounded natural-language answers through a REST API and an MCP tool surface
compatible with AI agent frameworks.

**What this platform is not:**  
- A regulated medical device or clinical decision support system approved for diagnostic use
- A production Electronic Health Record (EHR) or Clinical Data Repository (CDR)
- A replacement for clinician judgment

All data processed by the local stack is **synthetic** (generated by `producer/produce_events.py`).
Any deployment against real patient data requires separate privacy, security, and regulatory
review outside the scope of this repository.

---

## 2. Business Problem Statement

Healthcare organizations generate continuous streams of heterogeneous clinical data across
multiple source systems: EHR notes, laboratory results, device telemetry, pharmacy orders,
and claims. Extracting actionable intelligence from this data faces three compounding
problems:

| Problem | Business impact |
|---------|----------------|
| **Data latency** â€” Batch analytics run nightly or weekly. By the time a report is available, the clinical window for intervention has often closed. | Delayed deterioration detection; missed escalation opportunities |
| **Relationship blindness** â€” Traditional search and analytics treat events in isolation. Drug interactions, lab-to-condition signals, and patient journey context are invisible without explicit relationship modeling. | Missed adverse drug events; incomplete clinical reasoning |
| **Retrieval-generation gap** â€” Large language models generate fluent answers but hallucinate when facts are not grounded in evidence. Healthcare cannot tolerate unverified clinical assertions. | Eroded clinician trust; potential patient safety risk |

This platform addresses all three by combining:
- **Streaming ingestion** (events queryable within seconds of arrival)
- **Hybrid evidence storage** (vector similarity + graph relationship reasoning)
- **Grounded generation** (LLM answers cite the specific vector hits and graph context that supported them)

## 4. Business Capabilities

### 4.1 Real-Time Clinical Event Intelligence

Events are processable and queryable within seconds of arrival â€” not hours or days. The
streaming pipeline (Kafka â†’ Flink â†’ dual sinks) provides:

- **Event freshness guarantee:** new clinical notes, lab results, medication orders, and
  device readings are embedded in Qdrant and written to the Neo4j graph within a single
  Flink pipeline execution
- **Reference-data enrichment:** patient demographics, provider specialty, device metadata,
  medication drug class, and payer plan are fused with transactional events before
  persistence, enriching both evidence stores simultaneously

### 4.2 Patient Journey Traversal

The Neo4j graph model is patient-centric. Every query begins at a `Patient` node and
traverses typed relationships that mirror real-world clinical causality:

- A patient's full condition history, symptoms, lab observations, medication orders, device
  readings, claims, providers, and payer coverage are reachable in a single Cypher hop
- Episode-of-care scope (`Encounter`) and treating provider (`Encounter-[:SEEN_BY]â†’Provider`)
  link events to their clinical context
- ICD-10 coding completeness can be queried: conditions present in clinical notes that lack
  a `CODED_AS` edge to an `ICD10Code` node represent a potential coding gap

### 4.3 Drug Safety and Pharmacovigilance

Aligned with the [FDA FAERS model](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/fdas-adverse-event-reporting-system-faers),
three layers of drug safety intelligence operate continuously:

#### Drug-drug interactions (`INTERACTS_WITH`)

15 seeded interaction pairs with `risk`, `severity`, and pharmacological `mechanism`
annotations. Examples:

| Drug A | Drug B | Risk | Severity | Mechanism |
|--------|--------|------|---------|-----------|
| Warfarin | Aspirin | bleeding_risk | high | additive_anticoagulation_and_antiplatelet |
| Warfarin | Azithromycin | bleeding_risk | high | CYP3A4_inhibition_increases_warfarin_exposure |
| Clopidogrel | Omeprazole | reduced_antiplatelet_efficacy | moderate | CYP2C19_inhibition |
| Morphine | Gabapentin | respiratory_depression | high | additive_CNS_depression |
| Metoprolol | Insulin Glargine | hypoglycemia_masking | moderate | beta_blockade_masks_tachycardia_warning_sign |

#### Known adverse reactions (`HAS_KNOWN_REACTION`)

20+ seeded medication-to-symptom edges with MedDRA term and severity. When a `CLINICAL_NOTE`
event documents a symptom that matches a `HAS_KNOWN_REACTION` edge for a medication the
patient is currently ordered, an `AdverseEvent` node is automatically created and linked
to the patient, the medication, and the triggering event.

#### Contraindications (`CONTRAINDICATED_FOR`)

11 medication-to-condition contraindication rules. When a patient has both the
contraindicated condition and an active medication order, the contraindication surfaces
in every graph context query response under the `contraindications` field.

| Medication | Contraindicated condition | Clinical reason |
|-----------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Metformin | Chronic Kidney Disease | Lactic acidosis risk |
| Lisinopril | Hyperkalemia | ACE inhibitor worsens hyperkalemia |
| Vancomycin | Chronic Kidney Disease | Nephrotoxicity |
| Morphine | Chronic Kidney Disease | Active metabolite accumulation |
| Warfarin | Anemia | Increased bleeding risk |

#### FAERS adverse outcome vocabulary (6 codes)

Hospitalisation-related claims (CPT codes 99232, 99285, 99291, 99223 or institutional
claim type) automatically receive a `(Claim)-[:RESULTED_IN]->(AdverseOutcome {code: "HO"})`
edge, enabling queries like "how many patients were hospitalised following this medication?"

### 4.4 Lab-to-Condition Signal Detection

14 clinical threshold rules fire deterministically on every `LAB_RESULT` event:

| Lab | Business signal |
|-----|----------------|
| Potassium â‰¥ 5.5 mmol/L | Hyperkalemia alert â€” review ACE inhibitors and potassium-sparing diuretics |
| Troponin I > 0.04 ng/mL | Potential acute MI â€” urgent cardiac review |
| HbA1c â‰¥ 6.5 % | Diabetes Mellitus indicator â€” review antidiabetic medications |
| eGFR < 60 mL/min | Chronic Kidney Disease â€” review Metformin, Vancomycin, Morphine contraindications |
| WBC > 11.0 Ã— 10Â³/ÂµL | Infection signal â€” correlate with clinical notes and vitals |
| INR > 3.0 | Supratherapeutic anticoagulation â€” review Warfarin dose and interactions |
| LDL > 130 mg/dL | Hyperlipidemia â€” review statin therapy |

Each signal creates a `(Observation)-[:MAY_INDICATE {reason}]->(Condition)` edge, making it
traversable by graph queries and surfaced in every API response under `lab_signals`.

### 4.5 Revenue Cycle Intelligence

Claims events carry full financial context (`billed_amount`, `allowed_amount`,
`claim_type`, `service_date`, `diagnosis_code`), linked to `Procedure` and `Payer` nodes
in the graph. This enables:

- **Coding gap analysis:** conditions documented in clinical notes but without a matching
  ICD-10 code in the graph may indicate a coding opportunity
- **Claims-to-clinical traceability:** a denied claim can be traced back through
  `Claim â†’ ClinicalEvent â†’ Patient â†’ Condition` to the originating clinical documentation
- **Billed vs allowed analysis:** direct field comparison on the `Claim` node

### 4.6 Device and Remote Monitoring

Device telemetry events carry `device_type`, `temperature_c`, `respiratory_rate`,
`glucose_mg_dl`, and a categorical `alert` field (`tachycardia`, `hypoxia`,
`hypertension`, `bradycardia`). Alerts are:

- Stored on `DeviceReading.alert` in the graph
- Surfaced in `graph_context.vitals` for every API response
- Usable as filters in pharmacovigilance queries (e.g. "tachycardia alert + Albuterol
  order â†’ known `HAS_KNOWN_REACTION {meddra_term: Palpitation}` edge")

## 5. Business Use Case Catalogue

### UC-01: Bedside clinical decision support

**Who:** Attending physician, charge nurse  
**Trigger:** Patient status change or pre-round review  
**Process:** Clinician asks a natural-language question via the Provider Web UI or an AI
agent. The system returns an LLM answer grounded in the most semantically similar clinical
events (vector) plus the patient's full relationship graph (conditions, medications,
interactions, adverse events, contraindications, lab signals).  
**Business value:** Reduces time to synthesise multi-source patient context from minutes
(manual chart review) to seconds.

---

### UC-02: Medication safety review

**Who:** Clinical pharmacist, prescribing physician  
**Trigger:** New medication order placed  
**Process:** Pharmacy event is streamed and written to Neo4j. The pharmacist queries the
system. The response includes `interactions` (drug pairs with severity and mechanism),
`adverse_events` (previously detected reactions for this patient), and `contraindications`
(conditions active for this patient that are contraindicated for their medications).  
**Business value:** Real-time pharmacovigilance signal at order entry, without waiting for
a manual reconciliation cycle.

---

### UC-03: Lab result interpretation

**Who:** Ordering physician, clinical team  
**Trigger:** Lab result received and processed  
**Process:** Lab event is streamed, Flink evaluates all 14 threshold rules, and any
triggered conditions are linked via `MAY_INDICATE` edges. The clinician queries the
system and receives the lab value, the indicated condition, and the patient's medication
context that may be related (e.g. elevated potassium + Lisinopril + Spironolactone orders).  
**Business value:** Automatic first-pass clinical signal that surfaces the most actionable
interpretation before the clinician reviews the raw result.

---

### UC-04: Adverse drug event detection and reporting

**Who:** Patient safety officer, pharmacovigilance team  
**Trigger:** Clinical note documents a symptom that matches a known adverse reaction  
**Process:** After `CLINICAL_NOTE` is written to Neo4j, `merge_adverse_event_signal`
runs automatically. If the documented symptom matches a `HAS_KNOWN_REACTION` edge for
any of the patient's active medications, an `AdverseEvent` node is created with the
MedDRA term and severity. Pharmacovigilance queries can then rank medications by adverse
event signal count across the patient population.  
**Business value:** Continuous, automated FAERS-aligned adverse event detection without
manual case reporting lag.

---

### UC-05: Hospitalisation outcome and claims reconciliation

**Who:** RCM analyst, case manager  
**Trigger:** Institutional claim submitted or denied  
**Process:** Claims event is streamed and linked to `Procedure` and `Payer` nodes. For
hospital procedure codes, a `RESULTED_IN` edge links the claim to `AdverseOutcome {HO}`.
RCM analysts query for denied claims and trace back to the originating clinical
documentation to identify ICD-10 coding gaps or clinical-claim mismatches.  
**Business value:** Earlier denial root-cause identification, reduced revenue leakage.

---

### UC-06: Device alert triage

**Who:** Remote monitoring team, bedside nurse  
**Trigger:** Device reading with an alert flag received  
**Process:** Device event with `alert: tachycardia` (for example) is stored on the
`DeviceReading` node and surfaced in `graph_context.vitals`. The monitoring team queries
the system and receives the alert correlated with the patient's current medications and
any known adverse reactions involving tachycardia (e.g. Albuterol or Levothyroxine
`HAS_KNOWN_REACTION`).  
**Business value:** Faster signal-to-action for monitoring teams by contextualising device
alerts within the medication and clinical history graph.

---

### UC-07: Coding completeness audit

**Who:** HIM/coding team, CDI specialist  
**Trigger:** Pre-billing review or audit cycle  
**Process:** Query returns conditions present in the graph (from clinical notes) with no
`CODED_AS` edge to an `ICD10Code` node. These represent conditions documented by a
clinician but not yet assigned a billable ICD-10 code.  
**Business value:** Reduces undercoding risk and supports clinical documentation
improvement (CDI) workflows.

---

### UC-08: Population cohort risk stratification

**Who:** Population health director, care manager  
**Trigger:** Routine or ad-hoc cohort review  
**Process:** Unfiltered (no `patient_id`) vector search returns events from multiple
patients with semantic similarity to the query. Graph context is then fetched for all
returned patient IDs simultaneously, enabling cross-patient analysis of condition
prevalence, medication patterns, and risk tiers.  
**Business value:** Scalable, query-driven cohort identification without requiring
pre-built analytics reports.

## 6. Business Rules Implemented

Business rules that are encoded in the platform and enforced automatically:

| Rule ID | Rule | Where enforced |
|---------|------|---------------|
| BR-01 | A lab result that crosses a clinical threshold creates a condition signal | `merge_lab_signals` in Flink; 14 thresholds |
| BR-02 | A documented symptom that matches a known adverse reaction for a current medication creates an adverse event | `merge_adverse_event_signal` in Flink |
| BR-03 | A drug-drug interaction is flagged when both drugs are on active orders for the same patient | `graph_context` query + `INTERACTS_WITH` graph edges |
| BR-04 | A contraindication is flagged when a patient has both the condition and an active order for the contraindicated medication | `graph_context` query + `CONTRAINDICATED_FOR` graph edges |
| BR-05 | An institutional or hospital procedure claim receives a hospitalisation outcome flag | `merge_claim` CALL block; CPT codes 99232, 99285, 99291, 99223 |
| BR-06 | Vector text evidence is redacted from generation-role API responses | `_build_query_response` guardrail; `evidence_access_level: none` |
| BR-07 | Every API and MCP tool call is logged to the audit trail with tool name, caller role, patient scope, outcome, and latency | `_execute_with_audit` wrapper; JSON append-only audit log |
| BR-08 | API responses are capped at a configurable byte budget | `_apply_response_budget` trimming function |
| BR-09 | Tool access is role-gated; `read_only` role cannot call generation tools | `_authorize` + `tool_policies.json` |
| BR-10 | Raw event payloads are never returned to `generation`-role callers | `include_raw_payload` silently denied for non-export roles |

## 8. AI and LLM Governance

### Model strategy

The platform is local-first: Ollama provides LLM inference without an external API
dependency. This ensures:

- No patient data (even synthetic) is transmitted to a third-party API by default
- Predictable inference costs (no per-token fees)
- Model selection is operator-controlled via `OLLAMA_MODEL`

Anthropic/OpenAI routing is documented as a planned extension (ADR-0003) but is not
active in the current implementation.

### Guardrails enforced at the API layer

| Control | Mechanism | Business rationale |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Evidence text redaction | `generation` role receives `text_redacted: true` | Prevents raw clinical text from leaking through generation responses |
| Response byte budget | `RAG_API_MAX_RESPONSE_BYTES` (default 50 KB) | Controls downstream payload size for EHR/workflow system integrations |
| Answer length cap | `RAG_API_MAX_ANSWER_CHARS` (default 2 000 chars) | Prevents unbounded LLM generation from dominating response payload |
| LLM timeout | `LLM_TIMEOUT_SECONDS` (default 120 s) | Prevents blocking API calls for slow model inference |
| Temperature fixed at 0.2 | Hard-coded in `ask_ollama` | Reduces hallucination variance; consistent clinical tone |
| Safety caveat in prompt | System prompt requires "Safety caveat" in every answer | Explicit advisory disclaimer on all generated clinical text |

### Advisory-only posture

All LLM-generated answers carry a required safety caveat and are labelled as
"clinical decision-support for synthetic demo data only." No answer produced by the
system should be acted upon as a clinical directive without independent clinical review.

## 9. Audit and Compliance Posture

### Audit trail

Every API call and MCP tool invocation writes a JSONL record to `RAG_API_AUDIT_LOG_PATH`
containing:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `timestamp` | ISO-8601 UTC |
| `trace_id` | UUID per request |
| `tool_name` | e.g. `query`, `evidence_bundle_export` |
| `caller_id` | `role:{caller_role}` |
| `input_hash` | SHA-256 of request payload |
| `patient_scope` | Patient IDs accessed or `"cohort"` |
| `outcome` | `success` or `error` |
| `latency_ms` | End-to-end latency |
| `response_size_bytes` | Response payload size |
| `error` | Error message (on failure only) |

### Access control

Role-based tool authorization is enforced at the API boundary via `tool_policies.json`.
Three roles are defined: `read_only`, `generation`, `export`. Callers declare their role
via the `X-Caller-Role` HTTP header.

### Data residency

In the local development configuration, all data remains within the Docker network on the
operator's machine. No data is transmitted externally except to the Ollama model server
(also local by default).

## 10. Operational Constraints and Limitations

| Constraint | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Synthetic data only** | The local stack operates entirely on generated synthetic data. Real patient data requires separate privacy engineering, consent management, and regulatory compliance review. |
| **Local-first LLM** | Default Ollama inference is CPU-bound on most laptops; answer latency varies by model size (llama3.1 â‰ˆ 10â€“30 s on CPU). GPU acceleration requires Docker GPU passthrough configuration. |
| **Embedding fidelity** | The stable MD5 bag-of-words embedding is deterministic and dependency-free but lacks the semantic quality of a neural model. Retrieval precision is vocabulary-overlap dependent. |
| **Graph completeness** | Graph edges depend on events having been processed by Flink. A patient with no lab results has no `MAY_INDICATE` edges; a patient with no medication orders has no interaction or contraindication signals. |
| **Production boundary** | `deploy/production/` contains deployment configuration assets. Source data systems, Confluent Kafka platform, and Apache Flink platform are managed independently and are out of scope for this repository. |
| **No HL7/FHIR support** | The current event schema uses a custom Avro envelope. Integration with HL7 v2, FHIR R4, or OMOP CDM requires an ingestion adapter layer not included in this repository. |
| **LLM is advisory** | Generated answers must not be used as clinical directives. The system is a decision-support tool, not a diagnostic engine. |
